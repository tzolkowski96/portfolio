About

I work at Indiana University School of Medicine as a data analyst. Most of my work is data engineering—building ETL pipelines that process genetic datasets, managing Looker dashboards used by research teams. Previous role at Telkomsel in Jakarta had me building geospatial analytics dashboards for subscriber segmentation across millions of users.

ASL is my first language. That shapes everything. I think spatially, process visually, communicate directly. No filler, no hedging. My brain is wired for pattern recognition and systematic thinking. Being autistic isn't a caveat. It's the feature.

I also write. Data journalism on Medium where I've published across 13 publications to 380+ followers. The kind of writing where I dig into datasets and find the stories the numbers are trying to tell—grocery prices, work weeks, political shifts. When others just look at spreadsheets, I see lifeline after lifeline.
