About

Started at RIT studying Communication Science, shifted to International Studies at UW-Madison, and eventually found my way to Information Science. That non-linear path taught me that the most interesting insights come from crossing boundaries.

Now I work with neurological imaging data for Parkinson's research at IU School of Medicine. The PPMI project involves synchronizing terabytes of data across multiple research sites while maintaining strict compliance standards. It's complex work that requires both technical precision and human understanding.

Being Deaf and multilingual (ASL, English, French, Spanish) shapes how I approach data problems. Different languages structure information differently, and that perspective helps me see patterns others might miss. I write about these intersections on Medium and Substack.

Currently exploring how Tufte's visualization principles apply to modern research dashboards and why Excel might actually be a better programming teacher than most tutorials.

Work

What I'm Working On

I spend my days wrangling neurological imaging data for Parkinson's research at IU School of Medicine. The PPMI project involves making sure data from brain scans, surveys, and biospecimens all talk to each other across 50+ research sites. It's like being a translator between systems that speak different languages – which, given my multilingual background, feels oddly familiar.
