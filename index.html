→ Business Problem

Salifort Motors faced high employee turnover rates affecting productivity and increasing recruitment costs. The HR department needed data-driven insights to identify at-risk employees and understand key retention factors.

▢ Dataset & Approach

Analyzed comprehensive employee data including:

  • Work metrics (satisfaction level, projects, hours, tenure)
  • Performance indicators (evaluations, promotions, accidents)
  • Compensation and department information
  • 14,999 employee records with target variable (left/stayed)

× Methodology

1. Data Exploration & Cleaning

Performed comprehensive EDA, handled missing values, identified outliers, and validated data quality across all 10 features.

2. Feature Engineering

Created interaction features, normalized numerical variables, and encoded categorical data. Identified key patterns in overtime and project counts.

3. Model Development

Implemented and compared Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost models using cross-validation and hyperparameter tuning.

4. Validation & Interpretation

Evaluated models using precision, recall, F1-score, and ROI metrics. Extracted feature importance and business insights.

↗ Key Findings

  • Top Churn Predictors: Overtime work, number of projects (>6), and low satisfaction scores

  • Satisfaction Threshold: Employees below 0.4 satisfaction had 90%+ churn probability
  • Department Variations: Sales and Technical departments showed higher churn rates

▸ Business Impact

ROI Calculation: With average hiring cost of $15,000 per employee and 98% model accuracy, implementing recommendations could save $2.1M annually by reducing turnover from 24% to 12%.

  • Workload rebalancing strategies for high-project employees
