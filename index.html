About

Started at RIT studying Communication Science, shifted to International Studies at UW-Madison, and eventually found my way to Information Science. That non-linear path taught me that the most interesting insights come from crossing boundaries.

Now I work with neurological imaging data for Parkinson's research at IU School of Medicine. The PPMI project involves synchronizing terabytes of data across multiple research sites while maintaining strict compliance standards. It's complex work that requires both technical precision and human understanding.

Being Deaf and multilingual (ASL, English, French, Spanish) shapes how I approach data problems. Different languages structure information differently, and that perspective helps me see patterns others might miss. I write about these intersections on Medium and Substack.

Currently exploring how Tufte's visualization principles apply to modern research dashboards and why Excel might actually be a better programming teacher than most tutorials.

Weird Data I've Wrestled

That Time I Predicted Who'd Quit Their Job

Built a model that was 98% accurate at predicting employee turnover. Turns out the secret wasn't complex - people who work too much overtime quit. Revolutionary, I know. But it made me wonder why companies spend millions on "engagement surveys" when they could just... look at timesheets?

The Insight:

Overtime hours were 3x more predictive than satisfaction scores.

7.7 Million Ways to Crash a Car

Analyzed every reported US traffic accident from 2016-2023. Biggest discovery? Most accidents happen in perfect weather. Not because sunshine is dangerous - because that's when everyone drives. It's a masterclass in "correlation isn't causation" that would make any statistics professor weep with joy.

The Insight:

Normalizing by traffic volume flipped the "dangerous conditions" ranking entirely.

The 12-Year Gap Nobody Talks About

Used SQL to dig through health data from 183 countries. Found a 12-year life expectancy gap between rich and poor nations. Also caught COVID's impact in real numbers: -4.75% globally, then +5.91% recovery. Sometimes data tells stories we already know, but with receipts.

The Insight:

The recovery curve was steeper than the decline, suggesting systemic resilience.
