About

I work at Indiana University School of Medicine as a data analyst. A big chunk of my work is data engineering—building and maintaining ETL pipelines—plus managing Looker dashboards. I've picked up some software dev skills along the way, but analyst is still the core.

ASL is my first language. That shapes everything. I think spatially, process visually, communicate directly. No filler, no hedging. My brain is wired for pattern recognition and systematic thinking. Being autistic isn't a caveat. It's the feature.

Path here: UW-Madison for International Studies, then a master's in Information Science. IT support, data management, a year in Indonesia at Telkomsel building analytics. Now I spend most of my time in Python and SQL, wrangling data pipelines and keeping dashboards running.
